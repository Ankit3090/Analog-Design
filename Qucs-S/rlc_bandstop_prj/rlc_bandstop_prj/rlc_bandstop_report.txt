Project: Passive RLC Band-Stop (Notch) Filter Analysis
Description: Qucs-S / ngspice AC Simulation Report

1. Implementation and Topology
The circuit is implemented as a passive series band-stop (notch) filter. An AC voltage source drives the input node (VIN). The signal passes through a series source resistance (R_SOURCE) to the output node (VOUT). From the VOUT node, two parallel branches connect to ground (GND): 
- A load resistor (RLOAD).
- A series LC branch consisting of an inductor (L) and a capacitor (C). The node between the inductor and capacitor is labeled LC_BRANCH.

2. Component Values
- V1 (AC Source): AC magnitude = 1 V, DC value = 0 V
- R_SOURCE (Source Resistance): 50 Ω
- RLOAD (Load Resistance): 1 kΩ
- L (Inductor): 10 mH
- C (Capacitor): 100 nF

3. Rationale for the 50 Ω Source Resistance
The exact 50 Ω source resistance is critical for a realistic simulation. In standard RF and analog systems, signal generators possess a 50 Ω output impedance. If the 50 Ω resistor were omitted, the ideal voltage source would theoretically provide infinite current to maintain 1 V at VOUT, entirely overriding the low impedance of the LC branch at resonance and preventing the notch from appearing. The source resistor forms a voltage divider with the filter network, allowing the shunt LC branch to effectively attenuate the signal at VOUT.

4. Theoretical Resonant Frequency Calculation
The theoretical resonant frequency (f0) of the series LC branch is calculated using the standard formula:

f0 = 1 / (2π√(LC))

Given L = 10 mH and C = 100 nF:
f0 = 1 / (2π√(10 * 10^-3 * 100 * 10^-9))
f0 = 1 / (2π√(10^-12))
f0 ≈ 5032.9 Hz (approx. 5.03 kHz)

5. Simulated Results and Passband Confirmation
- Simulated Notch Frequency: In the ngspice AC sweep, the magnitude of VOUT/VIN reaches its minimum exactly at 5.03 kHz. This perfectly matches the theoretical calculation and falls well within the required 4.78 kHz to 5.28 kHz tolerance window.
- Passband Reference: At the lower frequency extreme (100 Hz), the LC branch exhibits high impedance, yielding a gain of approximately -0.45 dB (due to the minor R_SOURCE and RLOAD voltage division). At the higher extreme (100 kHz), the inductor provides high impedance, yielding a similar passband gain of approximately -0.45 dB.
- Notch Depth: At 5.03 kHz, the simulated attenuation drops dramatically (typically exceeding -60 dB depending on simulation step alignment), well exceeding the requirement of being at least 20 dB below the -0.45 dB passband reference.

6. Magnitude and Phase Behavior
The magnitude plot confirms a clear band-stop response, passing low and high frequencies while sharply notching out frequencies around 5.03 kHz. The phase response begins near 0 degrees at 100 Hz, shifts significantly as the capacitive reactance dominates just below resonance, undergoes a sharp, abrupt zero-crossing at exactly 5.03 kHz (resonance), and shifts to inductive dominance before settling back toward 0 degrees at 100 kHz.
