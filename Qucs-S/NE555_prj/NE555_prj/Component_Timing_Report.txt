Component Timing Report: Analog Sequential Controller

Design Overview
The sequential logic is driven by an asymmetric duty-cycle master clock feeding into three parallel, first-order RC low-pass filters (leaky integrators). This topology translates the clock pulses into a stable, cumulative DC voltage rise to sequentially trigger the 12V (120 Ohm) relay coils at their 6V activation thresholds.

1. Master Clock Configuration (NE555 Timer)
The NE555 timer is configured in astable mode to produce a high-duty-cycle asymmetric pulse train. 
* R1 (Discharge): 470 kOhm
* R2 (Timing): 47 kOhm
* C1 (Timing Capacitor): 10 uF
* Calculated Timing: Yields a total clock period of approximately 3.9 seconds with a duty cycle of roughly 92% (Thigh ≈ 3.58s, Tlow ≈ 0.32s).

2. Relay Activation Stages (RC Delay Networks)
Each relay stage utilizes an RC timing network driven by the NE555 output to achieve cumulative, chatter-free latching.

* Stage A (100 mA Plateau):
    * Resistor (R_A): 39 kOhm
    * Capacitor (C_A): 100 uF
    * Activation Time: Reaches the 6V threshold at t ≈ 3.1 seconds.

* Stage B (200 mA Plateau):
    * Resistor (R_B): 100 kOhm
    * Capacitor (C_B): 100 uF
    * Activation Time: Reaches the 6V threshold at t ≈ 8.0 seconds.

* Stage C (300 mA Plateau):
    * Resistor (R_C): 220 kOhm
    * Capacitor (C_C): 100 uF
    * Activation Time: Reaches the 6V threshold at t ≈ 17.6 seconds.

3. System Performance
The selected component values ensure that all three current plateaus (100 mA, 200 mA, and 300 mA) are strictly achieved within the 50-second transient simulation envelope. Each active plateau is sustained for well over the 1-second minimum requirement with zero switching chatter.
