Project: Capacitance-to-Voltage (C/V) Converter
Topology: Inverting Op-Amp Current-to-Voltage (Virtual Ground)

Target Scale Factor: 5 mV/nF
Op-Amp Model: UniversalOpamp (Finite-gain, low-input-bias approximation suitable for C/V conversion)
Supply Rails: ±15 V

Component Values:
Rf = 1 MΩ (Feedback resistor for sensitivity)
Cf = 10 pF (Feedback capacitor for stability and high-frequency noise limitation)
RL = 10 kΩ (Load resistor)
ramp_slope = 5 V/s (Vin implemented as PWL from 0V at 0s to 10V at 2s)

Sweep Values (Cx):
1 nF, 10 nF, 50 nF, 100 nF, 250 nF, 500 nF, 750 nF, 1 µF

Conversion Factor Calculation:
I_Cx = Cx * |dVin/dt|
Vout = -I_Cx * Rf = -Cx * Rf * |dVin/dt|
Scale Factor = Rf * |dVin/dt| = 1,000,000 Ω * 5 V/s = 5,000,000 V/F = 5 mV/nF

Sign Convention:
Due to the inverting topology, a positive input ramp yields a negative output voltage. Magnitude is used for error comparison.

Expected vs. Measured Magnitudes:

Cx = 10 nF  -> Expected: 50 mV    | Measured: 50 mV    | Error: 0%

Cx = 100 nF -> Expected: 500 mV   | Measured: 500 mV   | Error: 0%

Measurement Interval: 
Transient simulation runs for 2.5 s. The ramp is active from 0.0 s to 2.0 s. The measurement window is established as 1.5 s to 2.0 s to ensure fully settled, stable displacement current.

Observations:
- Clipping: None observed. The maximum output magnitude is 5 V (at 1 µF), which is well within the ±15 V supply rails.
- Stability: System remains stable. Cf provides adequate phase margin.
- Leakage/Bias: UniversalOpamp model configured for low-input-bias approximation; no noticeable offset voltages introduced in the measurement window.
